## Current Objective

The immediate research objective is now to work from a fixed explicit completion, a fixed reduced system, a first local theorem, a coercive longer-time bootstrap, a same-branch relaxation test, and a strict-branch decay test rather than from an open-ended setup question. The broader admissible-background theorem, the obstruction note, the summable-patch-control negative test, the nonlinear-stability setup note, the explicit-completion reduced-system note, the first local well-posedness/continuation note, the coercive bootstrap note, the strict elliptic-assumption relaxation test, and the strict-branch decay/obstruction note now all exist. The quartic branch therefore has a disciplined theorem endpoint, a disciplined first stability target, one definite nonlinear branch, one definite local theorem on that branch, one first longer-time estimate on the same branch, and one first identified obstruction to a straightforward global stability upgrade.

The next major burden is now more specific again. The first obstruction was isolated as the Coulombic lapse tail feeding back through \(\sigma_0(N-1)\), and the follow-on test recorded in [aether_flow_substrate_higher_derivative_strict_branch_renormalized_asymptotic_failure.tex](/Volumes/P-SSD/The Æther/aether_flow_substrate_higher_derivative_strict_branch_renormalized_asymptotic_failure.tex) shows that the recorded same-branch scalar-variable repair reintroduces a secular compact quartic source in the \(\pi_\sigma\) equation. The immediate decision that remained after that failure was then made in [aether_flow_substrate_higher_derivative_unit_lapse_reduced_system.tex](/Volumes/P-SSD/The Æther/aether_flow_substrate_higher_derivative_unit_lapse_reduced_system.tex): keep \(S_{\mathrm{min},4}\) fixed, change the gauge first, re-derive the reduced system, and test the old lapse obstruction at equation level. In that new unit-lapse spatial-harmonic formulation the old maximal-gauge lapse obstruction disappears at equation level because \(\sigma_0(N-1)\) vanishes identically. The first direct follow-on test is now complete in [aether_flow_substrate_higher_derivative_unit_lapse_trace_auxiliary_obstruction.tex](/Volumes/P-SSD/The Æther/aether_flow_substrate_higher_derivative_unit_lapse_trace_auxiliary_obstruction.tex): the straightforward unweighted decaying Sobolev \((K,\chi,\beta)\) package does not close because the longitudinal auxiliary solve \(\kappa_\theta\Delta_\gamma\chi=-K+\mathcal N_\chi\) generically produces a trace-driven Coulomb tail. The next two smallest repairs are now also complete. [aether_flow_substrate_higher_derivative_unit_lapse_trace_monopole_compatibility_test.tex](/Volumes/P-SSD/The Æther/aether_flow_substrate_higher_derivative_unit_lapse_trace_monopole_compatibility_test.tex) shows that the current unit-lapse compatibility conditions do not presently derive \(\mathfrak m_\chi(0)=0\) and do not yet prove propagation of that cancellation, while [aether_flow_substrate_higher_derivative_unit_lapse_coulomb_split_regular_part_failure.tex](/Volumes/P-SSD/The Æther/aether_flow_substrate_higher_derivative_unit_lapse_coulomb_split_regular_part_failure.tex) shows that subtracting the explicit Coulomb profile still does not restore the old unweighted \(H^N\) closure because a low-frequency inverse-Laplacian seminorm remains uncontrolled. The weighted/homogeneous reformulation is now complete in [aether_flow_substrate_higher_derivative_unit_lapse_weighted_homogeneous_closure_test.tex](/Volumes/P-SSD/The Æther/aether_flow_substrate_higher_derivative_unit_lapse_weighted_homogeneous_closure_test.tex), the next post-local step is now also complete in [aether_flow_substrate_higher_derivative_unit_lapse_weighted_homogeneous_coercive_bootstrap_test.tex](/Volumes/P-SSD/The Æther/aether_flow_substrate_higher_derivative_unit_lapse_weighted_homogeneous_coercive_bootstrap_test.tex), and the dedicated decay/integrability test is now complete in [aether_flow_substrate_higher_derivative_unit_lapse_weighted_homogeneous_decay_integrability_test.tex](/Volumes/P-SSD/The Æther/aether_flow_substrate_higher_derivative_unit_lapse_weighted_homogeneous_decay_integrability_test.tex): the low-frequency trace--longitudinal pair has the expected wave-type decay, the quartic scalar channel has the expected Schr\"odinger-type decay, and the quadratic and lower-order nonlinear terms are time-integrable in the same fixed-completion weighted/homogeneous class. The active next step is therefore narrower again but does not force a redesign: convert that consistent same-class decay/integrability package into an actual asymptotic or nonlinear-stability closure on the same fixed completion. The relaxation test has also clarified that the present ordered-flow positivity assumptions still matter structurally, so any weaker branch would continue to require genuinely new input rather than another small theorem adjustment.
